What is Lung Cancer Screening

Lung cancer screening is used to find lung cancer before it has spread.

Low-dose computer tomography (LDCT) is used for the lung cancer screening. The scan uses low amounts of radiation to get images of your lungs. It is not painful.

People who ARE screened

This figure represents 1000 people who ARE screened for lung cancer.

People who ARE NOT screened.

This figure represents 1000 people who ARE NOT screened for lung cancer.
Research shows that 21 out of the 1000 people who not screen would die of lung cancer.

With LDCT screening, 3 fewer people will die from lung cancer.
 

Risk

Exposure to Radiation

LDCT scans expose a person to radiation, which can increase his or her risk of developing cancer. We do not know how radiation from LDCT scan may affect a person's health.
